Dear Hiring Manager,

I am writing to express my interest in whatever position you happen to be hiring for, assuming it involves showing up, learning things, and doing a reasonably good job. My background includes glue production, elderly care, hammer-related labor, and a respectable amount of participation in various school activities.

Over the years, I have developed many useful workplace skills. At the Old Glue Factory, I helped turn a monumental number of horse hooves into glue while also identifying safety issues and maintaining strong awareness of questionable workplace behavior. At The Admirable Old Geezer Shack, I prepared meals, made conversation, and helped elderly residents with phones and laptops, which required patience, problem-solving, and the ability to explain technology to the technologically illiterate. At Hammer and Sons, I worked with power tools, reported safety violations, and was quickly moved into the advanced hammer division, which I assume says something positive about me.

I believe I would bring reliability, adaptability, and a glorious attitude to your team. Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to the possibility of contributing to your workplace in a (hopefully) useful and not dangerous manner.


Sincerely,

Cole Ethen
